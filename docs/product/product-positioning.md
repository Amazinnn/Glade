# Glade Product Positioning

## One-Line Positioning

Glade is a local-first life system that helps people record, recover, and gently understand their days without turning life into a workspace.

## Short Statement

Glade is built for life first.

It accepts structure, but it does not force people to start with structure. It accepts AI, but it does not let AI take over the meaning of a person's life. It accepts tasks, views, and future panels, but it does not want life to be flattened into rows, cells, and productivity rituals.

Glade should feel like life itself: open, breathable, grounded, and quietly reliable.

## Core Belief

Life should not have to become a workspace in order to be remembered well.

## Product Category

Glade is not best described as:

- a generic AI chat app
- a workspace for life
- a database-first note tool
- an efficiency dashboard
- a pure diary app

Glade is best described as:

- a local-first life recording system
- a long-memory companion for everyday life
- a gentle life reflection tool
- a system where structure grows out of living, rather than preceding it

## Who It Is For

Glade is for busy but sensitive people:

- people who want to hold onto life, not just manage obligations
- people who value memory, reflection, and continuity
- people who do not want to spend their energy building a system before they can use it
- people who want tools to support life without importing work logic into every private corner

## Primary Competitive Edge

Glade should not compete by being "Notion, but smaller" or "AI notes, but prettier."

Its real competitive edge is a combination of:

- local ownership: Markdown and SQLite keep life readable and durable
- natural capture: people can write first and organize later
- recovery of the past: notes must be easy to find again
- soft structure growth: tasks, themes, and reviews emerge from life records
- emotional posture: the product is calm, open, and non-coercive
- life-centered design: the product is about living, not optimizing oneself into a grid

## Product Promise

When someone uses Glade, they should feel:

- my life is being held, not processed
- I can come back to this later and still find myself here
- I do not need to perform organization before I am allowed to record
- structure is helping me, not judging me
- I can preserve something real in just a few minutes

## First Aha Sequence

The first meaningful Glade experience should unfold like this:

1. I write something naturally, without friction.
2. It is captured in today's Markdown and feels real.
3. Later, I can find it again.
4. Glade gently helps me notice what might matter: a task candidate, a recurring thread, a light review of the day.

## V1 Core Loop

The first version should stay centered on three things:

1. Today's Markdown
   The living container for the day. This is the factual layer and the trusted source.

2. Task Candidate Basket
   A soft structure layer that grows from freeform writing instead of replacing it.

3. Light Review
   A gentle understanding layer that helps the day settle into memory without overexplaining it.

Together, these create a simple progression:

record -> recover -> gently reflect

## Time Constraint

Glade should be designed around a practical time promise:

Within five minutes, a user should be able to record life well.

This is not a minor usability detail. It is a positioning constraint.

If a feature makes recording feel heavier, slower, or more ceremonial, it should be redesigned, deferred, or removed.

## Design Consequences

This positioning implies several design constraints:

- the interface should feel like a garden, not a control center
- structure should appear as support, not as mandatory setup
- search should mature before heavier AI automation
- AI should suggest, not dominate
- future cards, views, and panels should grow from life records, not from empty templates

## Relationship to Mainstream Tools

Glade should remain open rather than oppositional.

It can learn from Notion, Obsidian, Evernote, NoteGen, Blinko, and other strong products. It does not need to reject them. But it should stay clear about what it is protecting:

- life is not only project management
- memory is not only retrieval
- structure is not the same as care

Glade does not win by cutting itself off from mainstream note tools.
Glade wins by seriously serving a part of life that most of them only touch indirectly.

## Language We Should Reuse

Useful positioning lines:

- Not a workspace for your life. Life itself.
- Life first. Structure second.
- Built for life, open to structure.
- A garden for living, remembering, and gently growing structure.

## What To Protect

As the product grows, we should keep testing every major idea against one question:

Does this help Glade understand life more gently, or does it make Glade feel more like a workspace?
